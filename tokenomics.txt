• Initial Distribution:
◦ Private Sale / Seed Round (10%): Early
investment to fund development and initial
marketing.
◦ Public Sale (20%): Open token sale to onboard
the community.
◦ Development Fund (15%): Reserved for future
project development, maintenance, and upgrades.
◦ Staking Rewards (10%): Tokens set aside to
incentivize staking and yield generation for longterm investors.
◦ Team & Advisors (15%): Tokens reserved for the
project’s core team and advisors, with a lock-up
period to ensure long-term commitment.
◦ Ecosystem Incentives (15%): Incentives for
partnerships, liquidity providers, and ecosystem
growth.
◦ Reserve (15%): For future use cases, partnerships,
or emergency funding.
• Max Supply Cap: Implement a cap on the total supply
of tokens to ensure scarcity over time, which may
contribute to price appreciation.
• Deflationary Mechanisms:
◦ Burn Mechanism: A portion of transaction fees
(e.g., 0.1% per transaction) could be burned to
reduce the total circulating supply gradually,
increasing scarcity.
◦ Buyback Program: The project could periodically
use revenue or profits to buy back tokens from the
market and either burn them or redistribute them
as rewards.
 Economic Incentives
• Staking & Yield Farming:
◦ Encourage long-term holding by offering staking
rewards, providing incentives for holders to lock
up tokens for a set period.
◦ Yield farming could reward liquidity providers
with a portion of transaction fees or new tokens.
• Liquidity Pools & Exchange Listings:
◦ To foster liquidity, the token should be listed on
decentralized and centralized exchanges (DEXs &
CEXs). The project should incentivize liquidity
providers with a portion of fees or rewards for
providing liquidity on decentralized platforms.
• Referral & Community Incentives:
◦ Referral programs could reward users who bring in
new holders or investors, either through a small
fee share or additional tokens.
◦ Community-building efforts such as airdrops,
ambassador programs, or rewards for active
participation could drive engagement.
 Governance
• Decentralized Governance: Token holders could vote
on major protocol upgrades, ecosystem changes, and
other critical decisions. Votes could be weighted by the
number of tokens held or staked.
• DAO Model: Eventually, a decentralized autonomous
organization (DAO) could be implemented, giving
token holders more control over how the project
evolves and how funds are allocated.
 Integration with Future Coin
• Bridge to the Future Coin:
◦ The token could serve as a transitional asset that
eventually integrates into an established coin or
network, giving it utility beyond the immediate
ecosystem.
◦ Conversion Mechanism: Holders could swap the
token for the eventual coin (one-to-one or
according to a fixed conversion rate) after the
project has matured or reached a predefined
milestone.
◦ Token Migration: A carefully structured
migration event could take place once the coin it is
meant to become a part of is fully developed, with
token holders receiving a proportional amount of
the new coin based on their holdings of the initial
token.
 Security & Compliance
• Auditing & Transparency: Regular third-party audits
and clear communication regarding security measures
can help ensure trust and safety in the token's economy.
• Regulatory Compliance: Ensure the token complies
with relevant regulations (e.g., KYC/AML for token
sales, privacy concerns, etc.) and potentially work with
legal experts to navigate any jurisdictional challenges.
